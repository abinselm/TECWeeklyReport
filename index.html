        From 12.Sep - 18.Sep, 2025


IT Operations

  • Fifteen Yemen Mobile SIM cards were secured and activated for WFP emergency use, an additional 20 SIM cards may be provided next week, pending Yemen Mobile’s confirmation
  • Three critical internet fiber lines in Aden were expiring today, risking office connectivity. An urgent payment secured uninterrupted service until 18 October ensuring operational continuity while formal invoicing with Yemen Telecom is decentralized to Aden
  • Supported UNHAS team by installing eight workstations in their new relocated office building B as the new UNHAS hub
  • A new microwave link with a large dish has been successfully installed after reinforcing the tower on top of Building A with slings. Internet connectivity in the warehouses has significantly improved and is being closely monitored.
  • The tendering process for purchasing a new FortiNet network firewall has been finalized, and awarding suppliers is in process.
  • Aden IT team has resolved security complinces issues on more than 120 laptops, ensuring all devices meet WFP’s required security and operational standards.
  • Supported the colleagues in the SCOPE Unit by installing six new Ethernet sockets in Building B near the server room, enabling the setup of printers for scope card printing.
  • Coordinated with Teleyemen to diagnose, repoint, and optimize the Ku-Band VSAT connection at the WFP office, ensuring stable satellite communication.
  • Supported and setup conference meetings for the Management, Logistics, and Programme Units.
  • Supported ETC by dismantling ETC equipment from UNDP and transferred it to IT storage.
  • Provided remote support to field colleagues in Marib and Mukalla (Mr. Osamah and Mr. Abdullah Jassar) to prepare and configure laptops via Autopilot
  • Installed and configured a network printer for the UNHAS team, connecting it to the print server.
  • Set up desk accessories (monitors and peripherals) for Ms. Nino and Mr. Abdullah Ahmed, Head of HR.
  • Managed and maintained the EasyVista ticketing system, generating daily reports to track IT support activities.
  • The Network in Mukalla Hub has been monitored and to ensure continuous availability.
  • Staff in Mukalla Hub who experiencing M365 access issues because of the comline resteriction has been supported by IT Mukalla .
  • IT in Mukaalh has Enhanced the Wi-Fi coverage by deploying 5 GHz access points in the FAO Office, Operations Office, corridor, and the M&E and Nutrition offices.
  • Reviewing and updating the Wi-Fi Access Point patch panel and switch port mapping documentation in Mukalla hub for accuracy.
  • Conducted daily checks on printers and the server room in Mukalla hub to ensure operational readiness.
  • Preparing laptops for drivers and configuring access via the Virtual Desktop Solution.

ETC

  • Coordination was initiated with UNDP to dismantle and relocate the remaining ETC equipment from UNDP Aden office to WFP’s IT storage in Algwizi warehouse
  • Ongoing checking for Thuraya SAT phones functionality in Aden office.
  • Providing WFP staff VHF handheld radios using call signs as per UN standards.
  • Successfully maintained and ensured continuity of ETC connectivity services for UN hubs in remote locations.
